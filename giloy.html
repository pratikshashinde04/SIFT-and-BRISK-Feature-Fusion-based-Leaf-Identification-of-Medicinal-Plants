Tinospora cordifolia [गुळवेल]

Tinospora cordifolia, commonly known as Giloy or Amrita, is a perennial climbing herb widely used in Ayurvedic medicine. It is renowned for its immune-boosting, anti-pyretic (fever-reducing), and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular remedy for a variety of health conditions, including fever, infections, and diabetes. Giloy is also celebrated for its ability to detoxify the body, improve digestion, and combat stress.

    • Kingdom: Plantae

      Clade: Tracheophytes

      Clade: Angiosperms

      Order: Ranunculales

      Family: Menispermaceae

      Genus: Tinospora

      Species: T. cordifolia

    • Benefits Of Tinospora cordifolia

    Boosts Immunity: Giloy is a potent immunomodulator, helping to enhance the body's immune response and fight off infections more effectively.

    Reduces Fever: Its antipyretic properties make it effective in reducing fevers, including those caused by malaria and dengue.

    Improves Digestion: Giloy can aid in improving digestion and alleviating digestive issues, including hyperacidity and indigestion.

    Detoxifies the Body: It helps in detoxifying the body, purifying the blood, and fighting bacteria, making it beneficial for skin health and conditions like acne.

    Manages Diabetes: Giloy acts as a hypoglycemic agent and helps in managing blood sugar levels, making it beneficial for diabetes management.

    Side Effects Of Tinospora cordifolia

    Lowered Blood Sugar Levels: Giloy can lower blood sugar levels, which might be a concern for people with diabetes or those taking medication to control their blood sugar, as it could enhance the risk of hypoglycemia.

    Autoimmune Diseases: Because Giloy can stimulate the immune system, it might exacerbate symptoms in people with autoimmune diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, or multiple sclerosis. Those with immune system disorders should use it cautiously.

    Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: There is not enough reliable information about the safety of taking Giloy if you are pregnant or breastfeeding. Therefore, it's best to avoid use during these times.

    Digestive Issues: In some cases, consuming Giloy might lead to digestive issues like constipation or stomach upset, especially when taken in large amounts or on an empty stomach.
