Azadirachta indica [Neem]

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Neem, is a versatile evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is renowned for its myriad medicinal properties, including antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal benefits, making it a cornerstone in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. The tree's leaves, seeds, and bark are used in various forms for their health-promoting qualities, as well as in agricultural practices as a natural pesticide.

    • Kingdom: Plantae

      Clade: Tracheophytes

      Clade: Angiosperms

      Order: Sapindales

      Family: Meliaceae

      Genus: Azadirachta

      Species: A. indica

    • Benefits Of Neem

    Antibacterial and Antimicrobial Properties: Neem is effective against several strains of bacteria and fungi, making it a valuable ingredient in the treatment of infections and skin conditions like acne, psoriasis, and eczema.

    Dental Health: Neem sticks and extracts are traditionally used for oral hygiene to clean teeth, reduce plaque formation, and fight gum diseases due to its antimicrobial properties.

    Immune System Support: The extracts of Neem leaves, bark, and seeds are known to boost the immune system, thanks to their antiviral and antioxidant properties.

    Pest Control: In agriculture, Neem oil and extracts are used as eco-friendly insecticides and pesticides. They control pests without harming beneficial insects or causing environmental damage.

    Skin Care: Neem oil is widely used in skincare products for its moisturizing and healing properties, helping to soothe dry skin and reduce the signs of aging.

    Side Effects Of Neem

    Digestive Issues: Ingesting Neem oil or large amounts of Neem leaves can lead to gastrointestinal disturbances, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or constipation.

    Liver and Kidney Damage: Excessive consumption of Neem products can potentially lead to liver or kidney damage due to the presence of certain bioactive compounds.

    Allergic Reactions: Some individuals may experience allergic reactions to Neem, manifesting as skin rashes, itching, or swelling, especially if they have a pre-existing allergy to Neem or other plants in the same family.

    Reproductive Effects: High doses of Neem oil, especially when consumed, have been linked to potential reproductive and fertility issues in animal studies, although the relevance to humans at typical exposure levels remains unclear.

    Infants and Children: Neem oil should be used with caution in infants and children, as there have been reports of serious side effects, including death, after oral ingestion of Neem oil in infants.
