giant_milkweed

Giant milkweed is a large shrub that can grow up to 4 meters (13 feet) tall. It has broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of attractive white or lavender flowers with purple markings.

    • Kingdom: Plantae

      Scientific Name :Calotropis gigantea

      Common Name: Giant milkweed

      Family: Apocynaceae

      Subfamily: Asclepiadoideae

      Genus: Calotropis

      Species:gigantea

    • benifits of giant milkweed

    Wound Healing: The latex from giant milkweed has been used topically to treat wounds, cuts, and skin ulcers. It is believed to have antiseptic properties and may help in wound healing.

    Antimicrobial Effects: Some studies suggest that giant milkweed extracts possess antimicrobial properties, which may help in combating various pathogens, including bacteria and fungi.

    Cancer Treatment: Certain compounds found in giant milkweed have shown potential as anticancer agents, inhibiting the growth of cancer cells in preliminary studies.

    Soil Stabilization: The deep root system of giant milkweed helps in soil stabilization and erosion control, making it useful for land reclamation projects.

    Environmental Uses: Due to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, giant milkweed is planted for soil conservation and restoration purposes, especially in degraded areas.

    Side Effects Of Giant milkweed

    Skin Irritation: Handling the plant or its parts may cause skin irritation due to the presence of latex and prickly hairs on the leaves and stems,Contact with the skin can result in redness, itching, and inflammation.

    Gastrointestinal Disturbances: Consumption of giant milkweed may lead to gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, and diarrhea.

    Liver and Kidney Damage:Prolonged or excessive use of giant milkweed, especially its latex, may cause damage to the liver and kidneys.

    Reproductive Effects:Some studies suggest that giant milkweed may have adverse effects on reproductive health, including reduced fertility in animals.
